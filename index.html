Our Foundation

Driving Equity & Sustainability

Federation of Public Transport Sector (FPTS) is a national apex lobby group registered under the society Act Cap 108 that represents the rights and interests of the Public Service Vehicle investors in Kenya.
FPTS is the unified voice of Kenya’s public transport operators — advocating for policy reform, modernization, and a safer, more dignified system for all.

  • Influencing transport policy & regulations
  • Modernization and digital integration
  • Advancing safety, equity, and accessibility

Driving a safer, modern, and inclusive public transport system

News & Updates

Stay informed with FPTS advocacy wins, policy changes, industry developments, and media coverage.

Office of Inspector General, Douglas Kanja, Engages FPTS on Road Safety

July 3, 2025 | Jogoo House, 4th Floor, Nairobi

The Office of the Inspector General, led by Douglas Kanja, met with FPTS to discuss key issues affecting road safety and police conduct in the PSV sector. The discussion emphasized adherence to traffic regulations, fair enforcement, curbing indiscipline, and enhancing collaboration between law enforcement and PSV operators for safer public transport across Kenya.

Transport Committee Engages FPTS to Chart New Path for Kenya's PSV Industry

April 24, 2025 | Bunge Tower, Nairobi

The Departmental Committee on Transport held a consultative engagement with FPTS to deliberate on challenges facing public service vehicle operators. Key issues included gaps in legislation, taxation concerns, dysfunctional vehicle insurance systems, lack of training institutions, and poor infrastructure. FPTS proposed a National Road and Transport Master Plan to streamline investment, reduce operational costs, and improve overall efficiency across transport modes.

NaMATA Hosts Stakeholder Workshop for BRT Line 4 East

October 29-30, 2025 | Elysian Resort, Nairobi

NaMATA conducted a workshop on the conceptual design for BRT Line 4 East as part of the Nairobi Urban Mobility Improvement Project. Stakeholders including FPTS, SDoT, county governments, KURA, KeNHA, NTSA, KRC, and KEPSA discussed the updated design, urban mobility, and interconnectivity of public transport systems, aiming to enhance efficiency and institutional capacity.
